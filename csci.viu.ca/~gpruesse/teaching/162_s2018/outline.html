CSCI 162 (4) Topics in Computing Science
An introduction to applied and theoretical concepts in Computer Science. Topics include fourth generation languages, computer organization, system software, software engineering principles, and theory of computation. (3:0:1)
Prerequisite: Min "C-" CSCI 160.

"Useful links" may be added as the course progresses, at the discretion of the instructor. The "useful links" will not be a substitute for class notes. Class notes will not be made available.

If time permits, other topics may also be covered, including possibly: data storage, ADTs, networks, algorithms.

Although this is not explicitly a programming course, some assignments will involve programming in C++, Prolog or a "demo" assembly language. Students are expected to know all of the required C++, but other languages will be discussed in class.

Grading:

Grade = 25% Assignments + 24% Midterm + 3% Lab Participation + 3% Quizzes + 45% Final Exam
Alternative Grade = 25% Assignments + 20% Midterm + 3% Lab Participation + 12% Quizzes + 40% Final Exam The student will have the opportunity to write two more quizzes to help prepare for the exam. If it is to the student's advantage, the alternative grade will be used as the grade for the course.
  • Assignments

    • There will be assignments worth 25% percent of your final grade.
    Programming assignments must be electronically mailed to me. The program must be sent as an attachment and must be in ASCII format. Any programs sent to me in MS-Word (or any other format not readily readable in a UNIX environment) will be assumed to not work properly. In other words, failure to send me an electronic ASCII version of the source code for your assignment will result in the non-rebuttable presumption that your assignment does not work.

    All assignments, whether programming or not, must be submitted on paper.
    Links  to the assignments will appear on the CSCI 162 Assignments web page when they are available.

    Each assignment will have a specified due date, and any assignments handed in after the due date will not be accepted unless there is a valid reason for the assignment being late.

    Participation in the labs is mandatory.

    • You should attend the labs, because what you learn in the labs will enable you to do the Assignments.
    • You must attend the lab, because it is a requirement of Science and Technology courses that the student successfully complete the lab section of the course in order to pass the course.
    • You must attend the lab, because attendence will be taken, and a participation grade will be given at each lab. Non-attendence or non-participation will result in a zero for that lab. Each lab participation is worth 1/3% of your final mark, to a maximum of 3%. Not much, but if you fail it, you fail the course (see bullet point 2, above).

    Midterm There will be one midterm on March 6. It will count for a maximum of 25 percent of your final grade. The midterm will include material covered up to and including the class on Feb 14.
     

  • Final Exam
    • In addition, there will be a final exam worth 40 percent. The mark on the final exam can replace the midterm mark, if it is to the student's advantage.
    An old final exam will be made available and I will go over the old final during the review class on April 12.
    You must pass the final to pass the course.
