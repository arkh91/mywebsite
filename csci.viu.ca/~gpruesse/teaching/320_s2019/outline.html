Text:  The following is the required text for the course:

Introduction to the Theory of Computation, 3rd Edition (2nd is also acceptable)
by Michael Sipser. Thomson Course Technology. ISBN 978-0-534-95097

The following open-access, free online text will be used supplementary material for the course:
Theory of Computation by Anil Maheshwari Michiel Smid (U. of Ottawa)

Various other texts may be usefull to help the student understand the material, including the following book by Goddard, which has the virtue of being easy to understand and is a recommended read for those who find the material very challenging (though our treatment of Push-Down Automata differs from Goddard's):

Goddard, Wayne. Introducing the Theory of Computation   ISBN 978-0-7637-412-9 ISBN10 0-7637-4125-6



Content

The following is the general schedule for guidance only.
Week Topic Sipser chapter(s)
Week 1 Review of Sets, functions, etc.; Introduce Strings and Languages Chapter 0 and Chapter 1.
Week 2 Regular language; closure under union; Non-Determinism; algorithm for converting NFA to DFA. Ch 1
Week 3 Regular Expressions. Kleene's Theorem; Algorithms for converting among re, DFA, NFA. Pumping Lemma. Chapter 1
Week 4 Pumping Lemma plus Closure theorems for Regular Languages; Test on FAs [Jan 29]; Intro to CFGs Chapter 1.4
Week 5 Context Free Languages Ch 2
Week 6 CFGs continiued; Contains Regular Languages; Chomsky Normal Form Ch 2 (cont'd)
Week 6 Set Cardinality Pp 202-205
Week 7 Turing Machines; TEST on CFLs [Feb 19]; Turing Machines Ch 3
Week 8 Study Days Office hours are by appointment
Week 9 Formal Defn TM; Variations on the standard TM; Nondeterminism, multitape, etc. Ch 3 cont'd
Week 10 Church-Turing Thesis. Universal TM (not covered in Text). Decidable Languages and Turing Recognizable Languages Ch 4;
Week 11 Undecidability and the Halting Problem. Reducibility. Ch 4-5.
Week 12 Time complexity; NP-complete Ch 5. Ch 7.
Week 13 NP-completeness; TEST on TMS and Reductions (April 2) Ch 7 cont'd
Week 14 NP-completeness, Summary, Review Ch 7

Grading Scheme:

  • Assignments

    • There will be approximately 5 assignments; their total worth is 30 percent of your final grade.

    All assignments must be submitted on paper, and must be neatly presented.

    Each assignment will have a specified due date, and any assignments handed in after the due date, within 24 hours, will have 10% penalty; after 24 hours it will not be accepted unless there is a valid reason for the assignment being late.

  • Midterms and Quizzes
    • There will be three midterm exams; see the schedule above for the date. It will count for 30% of your final grade.
    The lowest midterm mark will be replace by the mark on the final exam, if that is to the student's advantage.
     
  • Final Exam
    • In addition, there will be a final exam worth 40 percent. A student must pass the final exam to pass the course.
