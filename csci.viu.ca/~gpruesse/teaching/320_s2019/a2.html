Assignment 2

Objective
To problem-solve within the particular constraints of limited computational models: Push-Down Automata and Context-Free Grammars.
To develop understanding of the models and to create algorithms.
To foster understanding of determinism and non-determinism, and develop the ability to critically assess, and to create, effective, rigourous, and concise arguments and proofs.

Task

  1. (10 marks) Prove that the following language is not regular, by using the Pumping Lemma. You can use the closure theorems if you wish.
    L0 ={ai bj| j ≤ i ≤2j}.
  2. (10 marks) Prove that the following language is not regular, by using the Pumping Lemma. You can use the closure theorems if you wish.
    L1 ={w ∈ {a,b,c}*| the first letter to appear in w is not a most numerous letter }.
    For our purposes, a letter is most numerous is there is no letter that occurs more often in the string. For example, if w = ababccba, then both 'a' and 'b' are most numerous, as they occur three times each in w, whereas 'c' is not most numerous, as it occurs just two times in w.
  3. (10 marks) Give a context-free grammar for the language L2 ={ai bj | 3i = 4j-1, i, j ≥ 0}.
  4. (10 marks) Give a natural PDA for the language of the previous question.
  5. (10 marks) Give a CFG for the language L3 = {ai bi+j cj | i, j ≥ 1 }.
  6. (10 marks) Give a natural PDA for the language of the previous question.
